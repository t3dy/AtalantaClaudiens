# FORSHAWREPORT.md — Peter Forshaw's Lecture on Atalanta Fugiens

**Source**: Dr. Peter J. Forshaw, "The Emblemata of the Atalanta Fugiens," Infinite Fire Webinar II, Embassy of the Free Mind / Ritman Library / GHF, University of Amsterdam, November 30, 2012. YouTube lecture transcript (499 lines).

---

## 1. Forshaw's Framing: Mytho-Alchemy

Forshaw's most distinctive contribution is his identification of Maier as "the main figurehead for what you'd call **mytho-alchemy**" — the practice of reading classical mythology as encoded alchemical teaching. He distinguishes this from:
- **Gold-making alchemy** (practical laboratory work)
- **Chemical medicine** (Paracelsian iatrochemistry)
- **Theo-alchemy** (Khunrath's theosophical approach)

Maier's *Arcana Arcanissima* is the foundational mytho-alchemical text: an interpretation of Egyptian and Greek mythology as concealed chemical doctrine. Maier's position is that if you read myths literally "you would be absolutely appalled" by stories of incest and rape — "this can't be the real meaning; you look for the secrets, and he says they are encoded alchemical secrets." The *Atalanta Fugiens* is the visual culmination of this mytho-alchemical method.

## 2. Biographical Details (Forshaw's Account)

Forshaw provides a concise but informed biography, largely consistent with Godwin and Tilton:

- Born in **Rendsburg, 1569** (Forshaw says "Rendsburg" rather than Kiel — a discrepancy with Godwin/Figala who say Kiel)
- Studied philosophy and medicine at Rostock, Frankfurt, and Padua; graduated M.D. at Basel
- More **conservative/Aristotelian** than Khunrath: "much more a follower of Aristotle and Galen, more traditional, more conservative medicine"
- Praised Paracelsus's chemical medicines as effective but condemned him as "a rogue and overly cynical"
- Arrives at Rudolf II's court in 1608; elevated to nobility September 1609
- **Alchemical coat of arms**: Maier petitioned Rudolf for a heraldic device based on Avicenna's image of an eagle (Mercury) and a toad (fixed earth) connected by a golden chain — drawn from the *Symbola Aureae Mensae*
- Goes to England after Rudolf's deposition (1611); possibly meets Fludd (same engraver, Matthaeus Merian, worked for both)
- Returns to Germany 1616; joins court of **Moritz the Learned of Hessen-Kassel**, the foremost patron of alchemy in the German states
- Moritz was also patron of **Johannes Hartmann**, the first-ever professor of chemical medicine at the University of Marburg (appointed 1609)
- Published 11 books in 1616-1618 despite being "physically ill"

## 3. Key Scholarly Arguments

### 3a. Maier is NOT Paracelsian
Forshaw is emphatic: "Michael Maier is not Paracelsian." The AF works with Mercury and Sulphur as the two main ingredients — the medieval dyad — without Paracelsus's third principle (Salt). "You can argue perhaps that the apple is the thing that really fixes Atlanta... but actually he never really gives any Paracelsian third principle all the way through the book." This aligns with Tilton's argument against reading AF through a Tria Prima framework.

### 3b. Chemical, Not Alchemical
Maier preferred the term "chemical" over "alchemical" — "he has a sort of reluctance to actually describe anything he does as alchemical." This is a significant terminological point for the early 17th century, when the two terms were becoming distinguished.

### 3c. Natural, Not Supernatural
Forshaw insists that Maier "explicitly says this is natural, it's not supernatural." While later interpreters (including Jungians) have read AF as spiritual alchemy, "Maier himself here is ultimately being very natural in what he's discussing." At the same time, Forshaw acknowledges "parallel processing" where Maier sees alchemy as mirroring his own life — like Ficino, he may have used alchemical purification and sublimation as a way of coping with melancholy.

### 3d. The Quadrivium Structure
Forshaw reveals that Maier structures each emblem's discourse around the four mathematical sciences of the medieval quadrivium:
- **Arithmetically**: "the root of the cube" (e.g., 2 as root of 8)
- **Musically**: "the disdiapason" (the double octave)
- **Geometrically**: "a generative point of a flowing line" (Boethian geometry)
- **Astronomically**: "the center of Saturn, Jupiter, and Mars" (cosmological placement)

This systematic mapping of each emblem across all four quadrivium disciplines has not been fully explored by other scholars.

### 3e. Maier's Cosmology
Citing Tilton, Forshaw notes that Maier's astronomical model was neither geocentric nor heliocentric but a "compromised system" similar to Tycho Brahe's, based on the work of the astronomer Rothmann who served Moritz's father Wilhelm of Hessen.

## 4. Emblems Discussed

| Emblem | Forshaw's Discussion |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Title page** | Detailed reading of the Garden of the Hesperides (top), Hercules as alchemist-archetype, Venus giving golden apples to Hippomenes, the race at bottom, transformation into lions in Cybele's temple |
| **I** | Boreas/North Wind carrying embryo = prima materia bearing the "fiery sulphur seed." Sources: Emerald Tablet (Hermes), Pseudo-Lull (argent vive), George Ripley, Scala Philosophorum. The embryo = gestation metaphor for the philosopher's stone (cf. Kitab al-Thalath Kalimat) |
| **IX** | Old man eating apples in a glass house (=alchemical vessel). Source: Ficino's *De Vita Libri Tres* on melancholy and astral magic |
| **XVI** | Two lions: winged lioness (volatile) and wingless lion (fixed). Standard alchemical bestiary |
| **XXI** | Circle-square-triangle geometric formula. Square = 4 elements. Triangle = body/spirit/soul. Circle = prima materia AND philosopher's stone. Color sequence: blackness (body/earth/Saturn), whiteness (spirit/water/Moon), yellowness (soul/air/Sun), redness (perfection/stone) |
| **XXIV** | Wolf devouring king = antimony purifying gold. Cites Lawrence Principe's identification from Basil Valentine's Twelve Keys |
| **XXV** | Dragon killed by Sun and Moon = Mercury destroyed by extracted Sulphur |
| **XXVII** | Man without feet outside the Rose Garden = impossibility. Source: Horapollo's *Hieroglyphica* (Egyptian hieroglyphic tradition). Also: Rosarium Philosophorum as structural model for AF's motto-image-epigram format |
| **XXXIX** | Oedipus and the Sphinx. Maier reinterprets the riddle: four legs = four elements, two = half-moon/white stone, three = triangle/body-spirit-soul. Cites Rhazes: "the stone is a triangle in essence and a quadrangle in quality" |
| **XLII** | Nature guiding the alchemist. The alchemist needs reason (lamp), experience (staff), and nature's footsteps to follow. Contrast with Francis Bacon's "raping nature" — Maier's approach is respectful following |
| **L** | Dragon entwined with woman in a grave. Dragon = fiery + earthy elements (Mercury). Woman = watery + airy elements (sometimes called "the Eagle"). Together = conjunction of four elements = philosopher's stone. Circular structure: final image mirrors first (Boreas/wind). Ritman Library's 17th-century painting reverses the order, suggesting cyclical repetition |

## 7. The Emblem Tradition (Forshaw's Context)

Forshaw provides important context for understanding AF within the emblem book tradition:

- **Alciato** initiated the emblem tradition with humanistic motto-image-subscription tripartite structure
- **Horapollo's Hieroglyphica** (pseudo-Egyptian hieroglyphs, discovered 15th c.) influenced the visual vocabulary; Durer commissioned to illustrate them for Emperor Maximilian
- **Rosarium Philosophorum** (1550) is "most likely the source that Michael Maier is using for his idea of motto, image, and epigram" — the structural model for AF
- Maier's innovation: adding the musical fugue as a fourth element (ears + eyes + mind)
- Emblem XXVII (man without feet outside the Rose Garden) directly combines the emblem tradition with Horapollo's hieroglyphic tradition of representing impossibility through a headless/footless body

### For the biography:
- Maier's **alchemical coat of arms** (eagle + toad + golden chain from Avicenna) — a personal identification with alchemical symbolism worth adding to the biography page
- The **quadrivium structure** of each discourse — arithmetic, music, geometry, astronomy — is a pedagogical detail not currently captured
- Maier's preference for "chemical" over "alchemical" — a terminological distinction worth noting

### For emblem pages:
- Emblem I: Forshaw identifies the **Kitab al-Thalath Kalimat** (Khalid ibn Yazid) as a source for the gestation metaphor
- Emblem IX: **Ficino's De Vita** as source for the apple/rejuvenation imagery
- Emblem XXI: Full color sequence mapped to geometric forms (black-white-yellow-red = body-spirit-soul-stone)
- Emblem XXIV: **Principe's antimony identification** from Basil Valentine
- Emblem XXVII: **Horapollo's Hieroglyphica** as source for the "man without feet" motif
- Emblem XXXIX: **Rhazes** as source for the triangle-in-essence/quadrangle-in-quality formula
- Emblem L: Dragon = fiery+earthy; Woman = watery+airy; their embrace = conjunction of four elements
