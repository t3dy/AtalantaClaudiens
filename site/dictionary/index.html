Dictionary of Atalanta Fugiens

Key alchemical terms as they appear in Maier's text, with Latin originals. 73 terms across 6 categories.

CONCEPT 11

Catena Aurea CONCEPT 1 emblems
Golden Chain linking Microcosm to Macrocosm; continuous thread of transmutation from base to divine.
Cauda Pavonis CONCEPT
The Peacock's Tail; an iridescent display of colors appearing in the vessel during the transitional stage between nigredo and albedo, signaling that transformation is underway.
Lapis CONCEPT 2 emblems
Shorthand for Lapis Philosophorum; the perfected Stone that transmutes base metals and heals all disease.
Opus Magnum CONCEPT 3 emblems
The Great Work; the entire alchemical project of transmutation from base matter to the perfected Stone.
Pelicanus CONCEPT
The Pelican vessel, a circulatory apparatus in which distillate flows back onto the matter below; also the symbol of self-sacrifice and self-nourishment.
Philosopher's Stone CONCEPT 3 emblems
Lapis Philosophorum
The perfected product of the Great Work; agent of transmutation. Achieved through union and fixation.
Philosophical Egg CONCEPT 1 emblems
Ovum Philosophicum
The sealed vessel containing prima materia; unity of opposites before differentiation.
Sapientia CONCEPT 2 emblems
Wisdom personified; alchemy as knowledge joined with practice. Central to Emblem XXVI.
Solve et Coagula CONCEPT 2 emblems
Dissolve and coagulate; the fundamental binary rhythm of the alchemical Work, alternating between breaking down and building up.
Tria Prima CONCEPT 1 emblems
Paracelsian three principles: Mercury, Sulphur, Salt. Tilton argues AF should not be reduced to this framework.
Vas Hermeticum CONCEPT 1 emblems
The hermetically sealed vessel; the closed system in which all alchemical transformation occurs.

FIGURE 16

Adonis FIGURE 1 emblems
Dying god of vegetation beloved by Venus; represents the philosophical Sun that must die seasonally and be reborn through love's restorative power.
Atalanta FIGURE
The fleeing maiden of the frontispiece; represents volatile Mercury that must be captured by the fixed principle.
Ceres FIGURE 2 emblems
Earth goddess of grain and nourishment; represents the maternal, nourishing principle that sustains the philosophical child through the stages of the Work.
Dragon FIGURE 1 emblems
Draco
Volatile spirit, guardian of treasure, and ouroboric figure of self-consuming transformation in alchemical iconography.
Hermaphrodite FIGURE 3 emblems
Hermaphroditus
The perfect union of male and female principles in one body. Represents the completed coniunctio.
Hippomenes FIGURE
The pursuer in the frontispiece race; represents fixed Sulphur pursuing volatile Mercury with golden apples as catalysts.
Isis FIGURE 1 emblems
Egyptian goddess who reassembles the dismembered Osiris; represents the lunar anima that restores the dissolved solar principle to wholeness.
King Duenech FIGURE 2 emblems
Rex Duenech
Alchemical king figure suffering from melancholic disease. Cured through purification. Appears in XXVIII and XLVIII.
Latona FIGURE 1 emblems
Goddess identified with the impure base metal (laton/latten) that must be whitened; represents matter in its unworked, impure state.
Oedipus FIGURE 1 emblems
Solver of the Sphinx's riddle; represents the alchemical adept who must decode nature's obscure language to proceed in the Work.
Osiris FIGURE 1 emblems
Egyptian god of death and resurrection; in alchemical allegory, represents the solar principle that must be dismembered and reconstituted.
Regina FIGURE 2 emblems
The Queen figure representing Luna, silver, or the feminine volatile principle in alchemical allegory.
Rex FIGURE 4 emblems
The King figure representing Sol, gold, or the masculine fixed principle in alchemical allegory.
Sphinx FIGURE 1 emblems
Mythological guardian who poses riddles and destroys those who cannot answer; represents the obscurity of alchemical doctrine that tests the adept.
Typhon FIGURE 1 emblems
Egyptian destroyer who dismembers Osiris; represents the corrosive, fiery agent of dissolution that breaks down the old form.
Venus FIGURE 1 emblems
Goddess of love who provides the golden apples enabling Hippomenes to capture Atalanta; represents the catalytic feminine principle that makes union possible.

MUSICAL 1

Fugue MUSICAL
Fuga
Three-voice musical canon accompanying each emblem. Voices represent Atalanta (fleeing/volatile), Hippomenes (pursuing/fixed), and Golden Apple (catalyst).

PROCESS 18

Albedo PROCESS 2 emblems
The whitening; second stage involving washing, purification, removal of impurities.
Calcination PROCESS 1 emblems
Calcinatio
Heating to ash; burning away impurities. Part of the washing/purification complex.
Circulatio PROCESS 1 emblems
Circular distillation in which the volatile is repeatedly raised and returned, purifying the matter through cyclical repetition.
Citrinitas PROCESS
The yellowing; intermediate stage of vivification between whiteness and redness.
Coagulation PROCESS 1 emblems
Coagulatio
Solidification; fixing the volatile. One nature coagulates only through dissolution of another.
Coniunctio PROCESS 7 emblems
Coniunctio Oppositorum
Union of opposites (male/female, volatile/fixed, Sun/Moon). The central operation of the Great Work.
Dissolution PROCESS 2 emblems
Dissolutio
Breaking down solid form into liquid; releasing the fixed into the volatile.
Distillatio PROCESS 2 emblems
Separation of a substance by heating to vapor and condensing, purifying the volatile from the gross.
Fermentatio PROCESS 1 emblems
Fermentation; the penultimate operation that introduces the ferment completing the Stone's transformation.
Fixatio PROCESS 1 emblems
Fixing the volatile; rendering the mercurial spirit permanent and stable through repeated operations.
Mortificatio PROCESS 2 emblems
The killing or death of the old form; the destruction of existing structure as prerequisite for renewal. Closely related to putrefaction and nigredo.
Multiplicatio PROCESS 2 emblems
Multiplying the Stone's power; the final augmentation that increases the perfected product's transmutative capacity.
Nigredo PROCESS 2 emblems
The blackening; first stage of the Work involving putrefaction, decomposition, death of the old form.
Projectio PROCESS 1 emblems
The final application of the perfected Stone onto base metal to effect transmutation; the culminating act of the Great Work.
Putrefaction PROCESS 3 emblems
Putrefactio
Controlled decomposition; the death of old form that enables new creation. Associated with nigredo.
Rubedo PROCESS 1 emblems
The reddening; final stage of completion, perfection, achievement of the Philosopher's Stone.
Solutio PROCESS 2 emblems
Dissolving of a solid into liquid; the return of differentiated matter to a fluid, undifferentiated state.
Sublimatio PROCESS 1 emblems
Raising the gross to the subtle; purification through elevation of volatile spirits from dense matter.

SOURCE_TEXT 15

Allegoria Merlini SOURCE_TEXT 1 emblems
Medieval alchemical narrative in which a king suffering from a wasting disease is cured through purgative operations encoding the stages of the Work.
Ars Magna SOURCE_TEXT 1 emblems
The combinatorial art attributed to Ramon Llull, adapted by pseudo-Lullian alchemists into a framework for understanding dissolution and coagulation as reciprocal operations.
Aurora Consurgens SOURCE_TEXT 2 emblems
Late medieval alchemical text attributed to Pseudo-Aquinas, fusing Solomonic wisdom literature with alchemical symbolism through a Christological lens.
De Lapide Philosophico SOURCE_TEXT 1 emblems
Alchemical emblem book by Lambsprinck pairing symbolic engravings with verse explanations, a key prototype for the visual emblem tradition Maier inherits.
De Veritate Artis Chemicae SOURCE_TEXT
De Veritate et Antiquitate Artis Chemicae
Sixteenth-century treatise by Robertus Vallensis defending the antiquity and legitimacy of alchemy, including an allegorical reading of the Atalanta myth.
Divinae Institutiones SOURCE_TEXT 1 emblems
Patristic work by Lactantius on divine wisdom and the nourishment of the soul, providing Christian authority for the identification of alchemy with sacred knowledge.
Fama Fraternitatis SOURCE_TEXT
First Rosicrucian manifesto (1614), announcing the Brotherhood of the Rose Cross and calling for a universal reformation of knowledge through alchemy and spiritual illumination.
Liber Sapientiae SOURCE_TEXT 2 emblems
The biblical Book of Wisdom (Wisdom of Solomon), part of the Solomonic tradition linking divine wisdom to alchemical knowledge.
Metamorphoses SOURCE_TEXT
Ovid's Latin epic poem of mythological transformations, providing the Atalanta-Hippomenes narrative that Maier adopts as the overarching frame story for AF.
Rosarium Philosophorum SOURCE_TEXT 4 emblems
Rose Garden of Philosophers; major alchemical compilation. Source for geometric, sapiential, and washing themes.
Secretum Nobilissimorum SOURCE_TEXT
Sixteenth-century alchemical text by Jodocus Greverus containing allegorical readings of classical myths as encoded alchemical teachings.
Tabula Chimica SOURCE_TEXT 1 emblems
Latin translation of an alchemical work by Muhammad ibn Umail (Senior Zadith), containing visionary imagery of volatile and fixed principles as paired birds.
Tabula Smaragdina SOURCE_TEXT 2 emblems
The Emerald Tablet; foundational Hermetic text attributed to Hermes Trismegistus. 'As above, so below' cosmology.
Tractatulus de Practica Lapidis SOURCE_TEXT 2 emblems
Tractatulus de Practica Lapidis Philosophici
Short Latin alchemical treatise falsely attributed to Aristotle, transmitted through the Artis Auriferae compilation. Source for elemental union imagery.
Turba Philosophorum SOURCE_TEXT 4 emblems
Assembly of Philosophers; major alchemical compilation of Arabic origin. Source for 9 AF mottos per De Jong.

SUBSTANCE 12

Aqua Regia SUBSTANCE
Royal water; the only solvent capable of dissolving gold, composed of nitric and hydrochloric acids. Symbol of the power to dissolve the noblest metal.
Aqua Vitae SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
The water of life; the purified mercurial water that dissolves impure matter and vivifies the dead body of the Work.
Aurum Philosophicum SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
Philosophical gold; not common gold but the perfected substance produced by the Work, embodying the solar principle in its highest form.
Elixir SUBSTANCE 1 emblems
The perfected medicine capable of healing all disease and transmuting metals; synonymous with the Philosopher's Stone in its medicinal aspect.
Luna SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
Silver; the lunar principle; the feminine receptive agent in alchemical transmutation.
Mercurius SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
Mercurius Philosophorum
Mercury; the volatile, feminine, lunar principle. Liquid primary matter, primordial water, water of destruction and resurrection.
Prima Materia SUBSTANCE 3 emblems
The first matter from which all metals arise; the undifferentiated starting material of the Work.
Rebis SUBSTANCE 3 emblems
The 'two-thing' (res bina); the reunited opposites in a single substance, combining masculine and feminine, fixed and volatile in stable union.
Sol SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
Gold; the solar principle; the masculine active agent in alchemical transmutation.
Sulphur SUBSTANCE 2 emblems
The fixed, masculine, solar principle. Paired with Mercury as the two fundamental alchemical substances.
Tinctura SUBSTANCE 1 emblems
The Tincture; the coloring agent of transmutation that imparts the golden or red color to base metal, synonymous with the perfected Stone in its active mode.
White Lead SUBSTANCE 1 emblems
Plumbum Album
Purified matter; the white earth into which gold must be sown. Product of the albedo stage.
