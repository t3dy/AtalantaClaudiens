Overview

Emblem VII bears the motto: "Ayoung bird came into being, flying up from the nest, And fellback into the nestagain." This emblem belongs to the citrinitas (yellowing/vivification) phase of the alchemical Work.

Maier's Discourse

Source Texts

De Jong's source-critical analysis identifies the textual traditions Maier draws upon for this emblem.

De Jong identifies the motto source as Senior Zadith (Muhammad ibn Umail), Tabula Chimica. The discourse draws on alchemical sources: Aurora Consurgens, Merlini Allegory, Pseudo-Aristotle, Tractatulus de Practica Lapidis, Lambsprinck, De Lapide Philosophico. The discourse draws on classical sources: Ovid, Metamorphoses X.560-704.

Alchemical Significance

H.M.E. de Jong: Winged = volatile/Mercury/Moon/female. Wingless = fixed/Sulphur/Sun/male. Elements bonded despite contrasts. Phoenix symbolism: resurrection through fire and dissolution.

Key Alchemical Concepts

Process: Fixatio, Putrefaction (Putrefactio)
Substance: Luna, Mercurius (Mercurius Philosophorum), Sol, Sulphur
