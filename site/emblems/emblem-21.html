De Jong identifies the motto source as Rosarium Philosophorum. The discourse draws on Pseudo-Aristotle, Tractatulus de Practica Lapidis.

H.M.E. de Jong notes: Rosarium formula: make circle from man and woman, derive square and triangle, return to circle. Four elements within unity. Body/spirit/soul triad.

Related Terms

Coniunctio (Coniunctio Oppositorum), Lapis, Opus Magnum, Philosopher's Stone (Lapis Philosophorum), Rosarium Philosophorum, Rubedo, Tria Prima

Scholarly Commentary

Hereward Tilton HIGH

Interprets through Aristotelian elemental transmutation: black Saturn/lead = body/earth, white phase = spirit/water, yellowness = soul/air, final redness = perfected unity of the stone. Not simply Paracelsian tria prima.
