Overview

Emblem XXXIII bears the motto: "Heisreceived into the bath, and heisborn in the sky, Buthaving becomered, hestrides over thewaters."

Maier's Source Texts

De Jong identifies the motto source as Turba Philosophorum. The discourse draws on Rosarium Philosophorum, Senior Zadith (Muhammad ibn Umail), Tabula Chimica, Solomonic / Biblical Wisdom Tradition (Proverbs).

Alchemical Significance

Paul Miner notes: Describes blackened hermaphrodite under waxing moon, engulfed in flames. Links to Blake's Gates of Paradise plate 5.

H.M.E. de Jong notes: Hermaphroditic imagery tied to transmutation of metals through balancing lunar moisture and solar dryness.

Related Terms

Coniunctio (Coniunctio Oppositorum), Hermaphrodite (Hermaphroditus), Nigredo, Regina, Turba Philosophorum
