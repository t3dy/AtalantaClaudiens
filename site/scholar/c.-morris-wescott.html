C. Morris Wescott

Music theory, alchemical symbolism

Musical-modal-planetary correspondences in the fugues; Alchemical King sequence

C. Morris Wescott is a scholar of alchemical symbolism and music theory whose article on the Atalanta Fugiens provides the most detailed analysis of the planetary-modal correspondences governing the fifty fugues. His work demonstrates that the three voices of each fugue structurally enact the pursuit narrative -- Atalanta as the volatile principle, Hippomenes as the fixed, and the golden apple as the catalyst -- making the musical dimension integral rather than decorative. Wescott traces the transformation sequence of the Alchemical King through emblems I, IV, XXIV, XXVIII, XXXI, and XLIV, complementing De Jong's source identifications with a layer of musical-symbolic analysis that her text-focused method does not address.
