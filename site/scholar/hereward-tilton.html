Hereward Tilton

History of alchemy, Rosicrucianism, historiography

Spiritual alchemy vs. laboratory debate; philosophical positioning of Maier

Hereward Tilton is a historian of alchemy and Rosicrucianism whose 2003 monograph The Quest for the Phoenix situates Michael Maier within the intellectual currents of early seventeenth-century spiritual alchemy. His reading of the Atalanta Fugiens emphasizes its philosophical and Rosicrucian dimensions, arguing that Maier occupies a middle ground between purely symbolic Jungian interpretations and the strictly laboratory-focused readings advanced by William Newman and Lawrence Principe. Tilton engages closely with De Jong's source identifications while offering a more nuanced assessment of Maier's Rosicrucian commitments than her work provides. His analyses of individual emblems, particularly XXIII, XXVIII, XXXIX, XLIV, and XLVIII, demonstrate how material and spiritual registers operate as inseparable dimensions of Maier's alchemical thought.
