J.B. Craven

Biography, bibliography

Maier's life, works, edition history

J.B. Craven was an early twentieth-century scholar whose 1910 biography Count Michael Maier assembled the first comprehensive account of Maier's life, travels, and published works. His contribution is primarily biographical and bibliographic: he gathered primary documents, traced Maier's movements between Prague, London, and Germany, and cataloged the editions and printings of the Atalanta Fugiens and other works. Craven's research established the factual foundation upon which later interpretive scholarship, including De Jong's source-critical analysis, was built. His treatment of the frontispiece engraving and edition history remains a useful reference point for understanding the publication context of the work.
