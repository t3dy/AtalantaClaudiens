Joscelyn Godwin

Musicology, esoteric studies

Musical edition and transcription of all 50 fugues

Joscelyn Godwin is a musicologist and scholar of Western esoteric traditions who published the first modern musical edition of the Atalanta Fugiens in 1989, making all fifty three-voice fugues accessible to performers and analysts for the first time. His edition includes transcriptions of the canons into modern notation alongside condensed summaries of the Latin discourses, providing a crucial complement to De Jong's textual scholarship. Godwin's work foregrounds the multimedia character of the Atalanta Fugiens as a unified emblem book integrating image, text, and sound. His edition remains the standard reference for scholars seeking to engage with the musical dimension of Maier's work.
