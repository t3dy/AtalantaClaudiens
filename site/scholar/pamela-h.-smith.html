Pamela H. Smith

History of science, material culture

AF as symbolic synthesis, not laboratory manual

Pamela H. Smith is a historian of science and material culture whose 2009 review of the Hofmeier edition of the Atalanta Fugiens in Medical History makes the influential observation that the work contains little evidence of laboratory or workshop practice. She argues that the Atalanta Fugiens functions as a synthesis engine in which meaning proliferates simultaneously across moral, spiritual, cosmic, and material registers, rather than serving as a guide to physical transmutation. Smith's reading reinforces De Jong's textual findings while pushing the interpretive framework toward the polyvalent, multi-register model that shapes current understanding of Maier's project.
