Paul Miner

Literary history, iconography

AF influence on William Blake; hermaphrodite emblems

Paul Miner is a literary historian and Blake scholar whose 2012 article in Notes and Queries argues that William Blake drew directly upon the engraved plates of the Atalanta Fugiens for elements of his own visual imagery. His iconographic analysis concentrates on the hermaphrodite emblems XXXIII, XXXIV, and XXXVIII, tracing specific compositional parallels between Maier's engravings and Blake's designs. Miner's work extends the reception history of the Atalanta Fugiens into Romantic-era art, demonstrating the lasting visual influence of its emblem plates beyond the alchemical tradition that De Jong documents.
