Walter Pagel

History of medicine, medical alchemy

Medical/humoral dimensions of AF emblems

Walter Pagel was a distinguished historian of medicine whose 1973 review of De Jong's monograph in Medical History brought a specifically humoral and iatrochemical perspective to bear on the Atalanta Fugiens. He highlights the medical register operating within emblems that De Jong reads primarily through alchemical and mythological lenses, drawing attention to imagery of dropsical ore, healing baths, and the balancing of bodily humors in emblems such as II, XIII, XVI, XXVIII, and XLVIII. Pagel's reading complements De Jong's source-critical method by demonstrating that Maier's symbolic language simultaneously encodes Paracelsian medical theory. His review stands as the primary argument for treating the Atalanta Fugiens as a work of medical as well as alchemical significance.
