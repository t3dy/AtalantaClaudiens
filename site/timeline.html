Timeline of Atalanta Fugiens

Reception and scholarship from 1568 to the present. 20 events.

1568

BIOGRAPHY Michael Maier born

Born in Rendsburg, Holstein

1597

BIOGRAPHY Maier receives MD

Medical degree from University of Rostock

1608

BIOGRAPHY Maier joins Rudolf II court

Court physician and secretary to Emperor Rudolf II in Prague

1617

PUBLICATION Atalanta Fugiens first published

Published at Oppenheim by Hieronymus Gallerus at expense of J.T. de Bry. Contains 50 emblems with copperplate engravings by Matthaeus Merian.

1618

PUBLICATION Atalanta Fugiens second state

Second typographic state (not separate edition). More widely extant; preferred by modern scholars.

1622

BIOGRAPHY Michael Maier dies

Maier dies in Magdeburg

1687

EDITION Frankfurt German edition

German-language edition published in Frankfurt

1708

EDITION Chymisches Cabinet edition

Vernacular German edition published Frankfurt. Later reviewed by Smith/Hofmeier 2009.

1910

SCHOLARSHIP Craven biography published

J.B. Craven publishes Count Michael Maier, the first modern biographical study

1964

SCHOLARSHIP De Jong article on Emblem XLVIII

H.M.E. de Jong publishes analysis of Emblem XLVIII in Netherlands Yearbook for History of Art

1965

SCHOLARSHIP De Jong reviews Wuethrich facsimile

De Jong reviews the 1964 Baerenreiter facsimile edition in The Art Bulletin

1969

SCHOLARSHIP De Jong monograph published

H.M.E. de Jong publishes Michael Maier's Atalanta fugiens: Sources of an Alchemical Book of Emblems (E.J. Brill). Foundational source-critical study of all 50 emblems.

1973

SCHOLARSHIP Pagel review published

Walter Pagel reviews De Jong's monograph in Medical History, emphasizing medical/humoral dimensions

1989

EDITION Godwin musical edition

Joscelyn Godwin publishes Atalanta Fugiens: An Edition of the Fugues, Emblems and Epigrams, first modern edition with full musical transcriptions

1991

SCHOLARSHIP Leedy reviews Godwin edition

Douglas Leedy reviews Godwin's edition in Notes, assessing musical transcriptions

2002

EDITION De Jong monograph reprinted

Nicolas-Hays reprints De Jong's 1969 monograph

2003

SCHOLARSHIP Tilton Quest for the Phoenix

Hereward Tilton publishes major monograph on Maier's spiritual alchemy and Rosicrucianism

2009

SCHOLARSHIP Smith reviews Hofmeier edition

Pamela H. Smith reviews German edition in Medical History, emphasizing AF as synthesis engine

2012

SCHOLARSHIP Miner Blake article

Paul Miner publishes 'Blake and Atalanta Fugiens' in Notes and Queries, connecting AF iconography to William Blake

2020

DIGITAL Furnace and Fugue published

Tara Nummedal and Donna Bilak publish digital scholarly edition through UVA Press with interactive text, music, and images
